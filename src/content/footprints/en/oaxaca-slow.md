Oaxaca is a city for slow mornings. The kind where you sit with a café de olla and a *memela* at a market counter and have absolutely nowhere to be. The quality of the light here is different from other Mexican cities — high-altitude sharp, the shadows crisp and defined.

## The Mercado Benito Juárez

Every hour here is alive with something: women in embroidered huipiles, buckets of dried chilis in thirty varieties, grasshoppers fried with lime and salt (*chapulines*) that taste better than they have any right to. I ate them standing, with a cold beer, feeling extremely good about life.

## On Mole Negro

A proper mole negro takes three days. You char the chilis until they're nearly black, toast the spices, grind everything on a *metate*, and cook it down, layer by layer, adjusting. The result is something that defies description — simultaneously bitter, sweet, smoky, and warm. Served over turkey with a stack of tortillas, it is, I think, one of the great dishes of the world.
