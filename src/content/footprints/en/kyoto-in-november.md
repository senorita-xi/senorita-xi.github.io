Kyoto in autumn is almost embarrassingly beautiful. The maple trees turn late and fast — one week bare branches, the next a blaze of crimson and gold that makes you feel like you've wandered into a hand-painted scroll.

## Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Go early. 6am early. The bamboo grove is extraordinary in the way that Instagram cannot capture: it's the sound as much as the sight. Wind moving through bamboo creates a hollow, percussive music, something between rain and applause.

## A Perfect Bowl

On my last evening, cold and slightly lost in Fushimi, I ducked into a ramen-ya with four seats and a hand-written menu I couldn't read. I pointed at what the man next to me was having. What arrived was tonkotsu broth so rich it left a film on the spoon, noodles that held their shape, and a soft-boiled egg that had clearly been marinated for days.

It cost 850 yen. I thought about it for weeks afterward.
