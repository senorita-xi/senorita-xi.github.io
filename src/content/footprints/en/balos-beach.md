<div style="text-align: justify;">
Remember those wild beaches in the middle of nowhere, with fine, white sand and crystal blue water? That pure love between the vast blue touching the hot sand... And no, it's not thousands of kilometers from here, just a step away. We stretch and imperceptibly the almost snow-white grains of sand slip between our hands, and the salt from our Mediterranean embrace with the turquoise sea is stuck on our feet. Here we are - on Balos, which we can reach with only an hour and a half flight and another hour or two "amphibious" and breath-taking with its amazing views to the lagoon, nestled in one of the westernmost points of the island of Crete.
</div>

## How do we get there?


### *By land* 
<div style="text-align: justify;">
With a quick check on google maps we can see that the distance is only about 50 km and is accessible in just over an hour. But honestly, I wouldn't recommend this option. From a moment on, the road becomes black - stony, sandy, and very narrow. And it can't take you directly to the beach - from the nearest parking lot above Balos (yes, it's above the lagoon), you have to walk another 20-30 minutes by stairs. Apart from that, the car the motorbike (which I hardly see as an option could be very easily damaged. And there is no possibility of repair nearby. Furthermore, when you reach the parking spot, you should climb some stairs( ~20 minutes walk according to your pace) at 35-40° after sunburn on the beach which doesn't sound like the most attractive option, well ... it is still possible.
</div>

### *By bus + a ferry*  
<div style="text-align: justify;">
Yes, this option is possible with the best bus company I have ever seen and used  -  KTEL.  It offers transportation to almost every corner of the island worth visiting. The timetable is available online. Buses usually leave early in the morning at different times tailored to your possible preferences. Return buses start at 4 pm, depending on the destination. Many people use the company's services, but there are always places. Before leaving for Balos, we considered buying an online ticket, but we weren't sure if we would wake up in time 🥱and decided to go a little earlier to the bus station (which is relatively central in Chania). We bought tickets to Kissamos port, where the ferry to Balos departs. As we started to board the bus, it turned out that ... there weren't enough seats. 😱Fortunately, the organization was accurate and after about 10 minutes a small minibus arrived, so we managed to get to the ferry in time. Speaking about the ferry ... We thought a lot about buying tickets in advance, but we were afraid of scams (several sites sell tickets, but they did not seem credible enough, and we are familiar with such cases when we bought tickets for the Statue of Liberty). So we decided to get tickets on the spot. There was a ferry at around 10:30, but there were no seats for it, so we took the next one at 12:40. There is a restaurant at the port where you can safely wait for the ferry. I recommend the ferry option because of the breathtaking views of the turquoise blue, the little boats, and the wind in your hair that is second to none.
</div>

## First stop - Gramvousa

The ferry ride offers this program - exploring the small, unspoiled island of Gramvoussa for about an hour before indulging in the well-known tourist destination of Balos. If you are a keen history buff, you have the opportunity to visit the Venetian Fortress, located on the top of the island, built in the 16th century by the Venetians during their struggle against the Ottoman Empire and one of the last captured fortifications on Crete.
![Crystal clear water at Balos Lagoon](../../../assets/fortress.avif)

And if you are a fan of the "favorite blue" I advise you to dive into the crystal blue waters of this magical corner of paradise. The beach starts with a shallow part, and suddenly it gets a bit deep, but on its behalf, you can dive and enjoy the presence of beautiful fauna and some remains of shipwrecks. Don't forget swimming goggles and water shoes as the water is extremely salty and the bottom is rocky.
![Crystal clear water at Balos Lagoon](../../../assets/water.avif)

We didn't go up to the fortress because we had limited time and decided to admire the sea and indulge in diving. The time passed imperceptibly, and the final destination was already waiting for us.

## Next stop - Balos Beach

Here we are at the magical place, hidden as if at the end of the world, welcoming with its silence and breathtaking beauty, its azure coast, electric blue, purity, and virginity.
![Crystal clear water at Balos Lagoon](/balos.avif)

Similar to Elafonisi, the beach is surrounded on both sides by the sea - on one side, where we arrived, the water is cold and deep, and on the other side, you can enjoy a real jacuzzi in nature, with a landscape of rocks and the smell of the sea

Like all good things, time flew by and the ship arrived. Our stay on Balos was about 3 hours.
