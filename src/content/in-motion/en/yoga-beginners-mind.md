I resisted yoga for years on the grounds that I wasn't flexible enough. This is, I've since learned, the equivalent of saying you're too dirty to take a shower. The point of yoga is not to already be flexible. The point is the practice.

## Why I Started

A physio told me my hip flexors were "catastrophically tight" (her word) after months of desk work and long flights. She suggested stretching. I suggested yoga. She said yes. This is how most things in my life happen.

## The First Three Months

The first class was humbling. I fell out of tree pose immediately, before the teacher had finished explaining tree pose. I discovered that my shoulders do not do the thing shoulders are supposed to do. I learned the Sanskrit names for positions I could not perform.

I also felt, at the end of that first class, genuinely lighter. Like something had been put down that I hadn't realised I was carrying.

## What Stuck

The Zen concept of *shoshin* — beginner's mind — is useful here. Approaching a practice without the assumption that you already know how it works, staying curious, not rushing toward competence. Yoga rewards this. So, I think, does most of life.
