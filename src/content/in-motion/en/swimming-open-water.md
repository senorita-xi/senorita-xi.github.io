The Adriatic in June is 19°C. This is cold enough that the first thirty seconds of entry require the kind of focused breathing that people usually associate with crisis management.

And then, by some physiological miracle, you stop minding. Your body adjusts. The cold becomes *clarifying* rather than alarming. You are suddenly, completely, nowhere but here.

## Why Open Water

Pool swimming is efficient. Open water swimming is something else. There are no lane markings, no push-off points, no tiled floor visible below you. There is water in all directions and the slight irrational knowledge that you are swimming in something much larger than you.

This, I have found, is good for the mind.

## The Ritual

I swim in the morning, before the boats are out. I leave my towel on a rock, walk in slowly — no jumping — and then swim parallel to the shore for twenty minutes. On the way back I usually turn face-up for a few minutes and look at the sky.

It is the most present I feel all day.

*Best spots: Vis, Croatia (June). Preveli Beach, Crete (September). The Lido, Venice — early morning, before it gets crowded.*
