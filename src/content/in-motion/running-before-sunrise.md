Three months ago I started running at 5:30am. Not because I wanted to. Because it was the only time that belonged entirely to me, before the day had any claims on it.

## The First Week

The first week was humbling. I had told myself I was "a runner" on the basis of some 5Ks three years ago. My body had a different memory. I ran four minutes, walked one minute, felt faintly ridiculous, and went home and ate toast.

But the 5:30 part — the pre-dawn empty streets, the streetlamps still on, the city in a state of provisional silence — that part I liked immediately.

## What Changes

By week three, the distance started to matter less than the state of mind. There is a point in a run, somewhere around fifteen minutes in, where the inner critic goes quiet. The problem you were turning over before bed is still there, but it's lost its sharpness. You're just moving through space.

I've started thinking of the run not as exercise but as *maintenance* — the way you service a machine, not to improve it but to keep it running smoothly.

## What I've Learned

- Start slower than you think you need to
- The second kilometer is always harder than the first and the fifth
- There is no bad weather for running, only the wrong jacket
- Headphones optional; silence preferable
