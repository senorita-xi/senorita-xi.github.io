*Past Lives* is the kind of film that doesn't announce itself. It arrives quietly, runs for 105 minutes, and then stays with you for weeks.

## The Premise

Nora and Hae Sung are childhood sweethearts in Seoul. Nora's family emigrates to Canada when she is twelve. They reconnect as adults over video call, lose touch again, and then meet once more in New York — where Nora now lives with her American husband — for the first time in twenty-four years.

That's it. That's the film.

## What It Understands

Celine Song understands that grief can exist alongside contentment. That you can love the life you have while also mourning the parallel life that didn't happen. *Past Lives* holds both of these things simultaneously without collapsing into tragedy or forcing false resolution.

The final scene is one of the most quietly devastating things I've seen in a cinema. I will not describe it.

## On Greta Lee

Greta Lee gives a performance of extraordinary precision. She has to convey years of feeling in small gestures — the way she holds a wine glass, where she looks when she's trying not to feel something — and she does it so naturally that you forget you're watching an actor.

**Rating: 5/5** — See it before someone spoils the ending.
