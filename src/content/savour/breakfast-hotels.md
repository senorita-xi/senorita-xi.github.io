There is a specific pleasure to eating breakfast alone in a city you don't live in. No obligations have formed yet. The day is entirely theoretical. You have coffee and something to read and a window, and that is enough.

## The Geometry of a Good Breakfast

A good hotel breakfast doesn't need to be elaborate. It needs: strong coffee, something baked and still warm, fruit that has been left to ripen rather than refrigerated into submission, and ideally a view of something happening outside. A street. A market. Pigeons disputing territory.

## Three Memorable Ones

**Dubrovnik, Croatia**: A small guesthouse where the owner brought fig jam she had made herself. The figs were from a tree in the courtyard. I ate four slices of bread.

**Chiang Mai, Thailand**: Khao tom — rice soup with ginger and a soft egg — eaten at a plastic table under a ceiling fan. The correct breakfast for a warm morning anywhere.

**Seville, Spain**: A tostada con tomate y aceite, which is simply toast rubbed with tomato and olive oil and a pinch of salt. Aggressively simple. Completely correct.

---

*The best hotel breakfast I've had was in a place with four rooms and no menu. You ate what they brought you. It was always right.*
