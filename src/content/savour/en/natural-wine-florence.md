I hadn't planned to spend three hours in a wine bar the size of a corridor. But the woman behind the counter poured me something orange and funky and said "you'll like this one" with the calm confidence of someone who is almost never wrong, and she wasn't.

## On Natural Wine

Natural wine is a contested term. It broadly means wine made with minimal intervention — organic or biodynamic grapes, wild yeasts, no or minimal sulphites, no fining agents. The results can be extraordinary or undrinkable, sometimes in the same bottle depending on the day.

What it almost always is: *interesting*. Conventional wine can be technically perfect and completely forgettable. A good natural wine makes you pay attention.

## The Orange in Question

It was a Trebbiano Toscano from a small producer in Arezzo. Macerated on the skins for forty days, which turns a white grape amber and gives it the tannins and texture you normally associate with red wine. It tasted like dried apricot and toast and something vaguely herbal that I couldn't name.

I bought two bottles to bring home. I have one left and I'm treating it with enormous respect.
